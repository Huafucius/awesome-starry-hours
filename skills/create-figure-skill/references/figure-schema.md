# Figure Schema

Use this ontology to understand a public figure well enough to build a reusable skill.

## Core dimensions

These 4 dimensions are mandatory:

1. **Mental models**
   - The abstractions this person uses repeatedly.
2. **Decision heuristics**
   - The rules they rely on when tradeoffs become real.
3. **Actions and works**
   - Evidence from what they built, wrote, changed, or publicly defended.
4. **Boundaries and unknowns**
   - Where the evidence runs out, where the person is weak, and where the skill should avoid false certainty.

## Extended dimensions

5. **Identity and biography**
   - Background only to the extent it explains later choices.
6. **Worldview and values**
   - Moral ranking, civilizational assumptions, and recurring concerns.
7. **Expression DNA**
   - Sentence rhythm, metaphors, irony, attack style, and explanatory posture.
8. **Relational patterns**
   - How they address peers, students, rivals, institutions, and the public.
9. **Stress and conflict**
   - How they react under attack, pressure, ambiguity, or disappointment.
10. **Evolution and contradictions**
   - What changed over time and what tensions were never fully resolved.

## Principle

The goal is not to perfectly imitate the person.

The goal is to produce a source-backed operating model that helps Claude reason better in that person's style of judgment.
